  • Our Mission

    To lead innovative, patient centered research, identifying neuroimaging biomarkers through interdisciplinary collaboration with scientists, clinicians and industry partners. Our goal is to identify, develop, implement and validate imaging biomarkers for brain disorders, leading to new solutions to clinical problems, better diagnosis of brain disorders and improving patient health.

  • Meet the Team

    Our team is comprised of physicists, neuroscientists, clinician-scientists (including neurologists, psychiatrists, neuroradiologists, neurosurgeons, and internal medicine)

    Dr. Tim Bardouille

    Assistant Professor of Physics & Atmospheric Sciences

    Tim is a neuroimaging scientist who leads the MEG research program in Halifax, and has been instrumental in driving the development of a combined clinical and research program in both pediatric and adult MEG for pre-surgical mapping.

      View CV

    Dr. Steven Beyea

    Associate Professor of Radiology

    In addition to his own research in the development and translation of medical imaging acquisition and analysis technologies, Steven is the Lead Scientist for the Biomedical Translational Imaging Centre (BIOTIC) a core research facility located in the IWK and QEII Health Centres.

      View CV

    Dr. Chris Bowen

    Assistant Professor of Radiology

    A physicist with expertise in the development of both human and animal imaging acquisition technologies, in particular using MRI as well as novel cellular imaging techniques.



      View CV

    Dr. Kim Brewer

    Assistant Professor of Radiology

    A biophysicist who focuses on molecular and cellular imaging technologies for elucidating novel therapeutics (e.g., cancer immunotherapy), Kim also leads the pre-clinical imaging research program at BIOTIC.

      View CV

    Dr. Cynthia Calkin

    Associate Professor Psychiatry

    A clinical psychiatrist, Cynthia’s research aims to develop and translate quantifiable diagnostic tools for better understanding mechanisms of disease in sub-populations of neuropsychiatric patients, which could be used for early diagnosis and targeting and monitoring treatment response.

      View CV

    Dr. David Clarke

    Professor of Neurosurgery

    In addition to his role as Chief of Neurosurgery (NSHA) and Head of Neurosurgery at Dalhousie, David’s research ranges from virtual reality technologies for improving surgical outcomes to mechanisms and implications for the role of neural cell adhesion in retinal ganglion cells.

      View CV

    Dr. Sultan Darvesh

    Professor of Medical Neuroscience and Sobey Chair in Curative Approaches to Alzheimer’s Disease

    Sultan is a neurologist with expertise in dementias, as well as an organic/medicinal chemist with extensive experience in organic synthesis, radiosynthesis and analysis of inhibitors and substrates of butrycholinesterase and study of human brain tissues.

      View CV

    Dr. John Fisk

    Associate Professor of Psychiatry

    A neuropsychologist specializing in neurodegenerative disorders, John’s research is focused on MS, dementia, systemic lupus erythematosus and Parkinson’s disease and examines the early cognitive symptoms of these disorders and associated neuroimaging measures, as well as effects on health-related quality of life.

      View CV

    Dr. Alon Friedman

    Professor of Medical Neuroscience and Dennis Chair in Epilepsy Research

    Alon’s research focus is on how the components of the neurovascular network, including nerve cells, glia and vasculature, interact to underlie abnormal brain functions following traumatic and ischemic brain injuries. Through a combination of molecular, imaging and electrophysiological techniques, his research focuses on shedding light on the unknown pathological cascade leading to post-injury epilepsy and neurodegeneration, towards the development of new diagnostics and preventive treatments in injured individuals.

      View CV

    Dr. John Hanly

    Professor of Rheumatology

    In addition to his role as Director of Research & CME for the Division of Rheumatology, John’s research focuses on the study of lupus, and has for many years lead a CIHR funded international, multi-center, prospective cohort study of neuropsychiatric systemic lupus erythematosus.

      View CV

    Dr. Javeria Hashmi

    Assistant Professor of Anesthesia and Canada Research Chair in Pain (Tier II)

    Javeria’s research is focused on neuroimaging predictors of pain response, in particular through the use of big data analysis approaches as applied to functional neuroimaging. This work includes the development of a clinical trial to examine brain mechanisms for the role of expectation in inducing analgesia in patients with chronic back pain.

      View CV

    Dr. Matthias Schmidt

    Professor of Radiology

    A clinical neuroradiologist, Matthias is also Director of Research for the Department of Radiology, and own his research interests involve the development and clinical validation of innovative neuroradiology techniques, in particular for epilepsy.

      View CV
